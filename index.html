How to use the workbench

Build: tap a gear size in the palette then tap the sheet, or drag a size straight onto the sheet. Drag gears near each other and they snap into mesh. Drop a gear onto another gear's hub to stack it on the same shaft — that's a compound pair, shown with the largest gear behind and the smallest in front. A shaft holds at most two gears.

Power it: choose the Motor tool and tap a gear to attach the motor — or drag the Motor button straight onto a gear. Do the same with the Load tool where you want to measure output.

Read it: the amber readout shows overall ratio, output speed, output torque, and direction. CW = clockwise.

  • Meshed gears turn opposite directions; gears on one shaft turn together.
  • Ideal train: speed ÷ R, torque × R. Power in = power out.
  • Gears only mesh within the same plane. Front-plane gears are light; back-plane gears are darker.

Challenge mode: build the train, then enter your predicted output before you run. The workbench compares your prediction to the actual result — calculate first, then check.

Remove & undo: with the Remove tool, tap a gear to delete it, or tap an M/L badge to detach the motor or load. Undo (or Ctrl/⌘ Z) reverses the last change.

Keys: Delete removes the selected gear. Ctrl/⌘ Z undoes. Esc cancels a tool.
